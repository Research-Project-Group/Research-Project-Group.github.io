Literature Survey

Construction is the one of major industry in Sri lanka. [1]

Figure 1. Revanue from the construction industry in sri lanka 2011 - 2021 [1]

The construction industry in Sri Lanka, a vital contributor to the nation's economy and employment sector, currently confronts significant hurdles in material procurement and transportation. To address these challenges, MaterialFlow presents an innovative model-based platform engineered to streamline the materials delivery process in this industry. This platform comprises four main components: Customer and product clustering, Supplier-Driver Pair Recommendation, Price Prediction and Discount Analysis, and Fraud Detection. Through the integration of advanced machine learning and data clustering techniques, MaterialFlow aims to optimize supplierdriver pairings, facilitate predictive pricing strategies, and combat fraudulent activities. Leveraging contemporary software and cloud technologies, the platform endeavors to enhance operational efficiency and reduce costs across the construction supply chain. This paper elucidates the system's architecture, delineates its functional and non-functional requirements, and outlines plans for future development and real-world application validation

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How to Addressing Procurement, Logistics, and Fraud Challenges through a Multisided Digital Platform?

The Sri Lankan construction industry is plagued by inefficiencies in material procurement and logistics, including unreliable suppliers, transportation delays, cost management issues, and fraud. These problems lead to project delays, increased expenses, and reduced competitiveness.

Proposed Solution

To build a reliable recommendation system for supplier-driver pairs, we will start by collecting and preprocessing historical data on supplier-driver interactions, client feedback, and order details to ensure data quality. Relevant features such as ratings, order completion time, geographic location, order types, and client preferences will be extracted. We will then implement collaborative filtering techniques (user-based and item-based) and matrix factorization methods like Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) to suggest and rank the best supplier-driver pairs, leveraging both implicit and explicit feedback from past interactions. This model will continuously learn and adapt to new data to improve recommendation accuracy and relevance.

Research Objectives

Develop a system to generate personalized product recommendations based on customer and product clusters.

Personalized product recommendations based on product clusters and customers are critical to improving inventory management and customer experience within the MaterialFlow platform. This goal entails a number of crucial actions and approaches that are in line with the general objectives of the study.

Use Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) and collaborative filtering to identify hidden trends and optimize pairings.

Improving supplier-driver pairings is essential for enhancing the logistics of material delivery in the building supply chain when it comes to the MaterialFlow platform. This goal is to increase the effectiveness of supplier-driver pairings by utilizing collaborative filtering techniques and Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) to find hidden patterns in transactional data.

Conduct predictive analysis to forecast retail and wholesale prices and determine optimal discount strategies.

For efficient inventory management and pricing inside the MaterialFlow platform, precise retail and wholesale price forecasts as well as the identification of the best possible discount plans are crucial. This goal entails assisting shop managers in making wise judgments that optimize profitability and raise market competitiveness by utilizing cutting-edge predictive analysis tools.

Apply clustering algorithms (K-Means) to detect patterns indicative of fraudulent activities.

In the context of the MaterialFlow platform, detecting fraudulent activities is critical to ensuring the security and integrity of the construction supply chain. This objective involves using clustering algorithms, specifically K-Means, to identify patterns in customer and transaction data that indicate potential fraud. By clustering data points based on similarities, the system can effectively flag unusual or suspicious behaviors that deviate from normal patterns.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed material delivery system consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. Customer Clustering System: Grouping customers and products to better understand and serve their needs.
  2. Supplier Driver Recommendation System: Matching suppliers with drivers to ensure efficient and timely deliveries
  3. Fraud Detection System: Identifying and preventing fraudulent activities to maintain the integrity of the supply chain.
  4. Price Prediction System: Using data to predict pricing trends and analyze discounts to offer competitive rates

In the customer and products clustering module, the process starts with gathering and cleaning data, including RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) value calculation for both customers and products. Then, K-means clustering is used to group customers based on their RFM values, as well as products. By combining these clusters, personalized product recommendations for customers are generated. In the supplier driver recommendation system, the process starts with gathering and cleaning data, including supplier and driver information. The fraud detection system identifies fraudulent activities by analysing the transactions occurring within the platform. The price prediction system uses historical data to forecast retail and wholesale prices, as well as determine optimal discount strategies.
